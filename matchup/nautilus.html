Early game: Nautilus is really weak early, get behind him and force him into the river to deny EXP. Doesn't really matter what he starts, if you can get in range just Auto → Flip. Best if you can time the flip to buffer his passive root, but doesn't really matter.

How to trade: This Nautilus champ does not belong in top lane, you can just Q→Auto W PTA or Q→E (better when he's not full HP) whenever he walks up to CS and you win hard. If he ever Qs, be ready to E buffer into his Q and you win hard. If he doesn't walk up to CS/plays passively, walk behind his wave and just deny him CS/EXP. He can't force fights on you because you can buffer his Q and you just stat check him.

What to watch out for: Jungle ganks are the only issue in this lane, as he has crazy CC. If you E buffer AWAY from his Q, the timing is a bit weird as it can sometimes cancel your E. You have to E as late as possible, if you do it a bit too early he can knock you down. Be careful of his E poke, but the range is so short vs your range it's not that scary — that's his only real way to trade favorably, if you walk into it.

Tips: Do whatever you want to this champ, might be easier than Singed top ngl. You can E buffer his passive, Q, and Ulti. You outscale in every aspect at every point in the game, just focus on not dying to jungle ganks and you'll smoke him in sidelane, 1v1, or 5v5.
